Flew the Coop

The best way to plan your trips and generate itineraries.

Type

Independent student project completed while in The Ohio State University UX/UI Certificate Bootcamp.

Responsibilities

User research, personas, sketches, wireframing, and prototyping to low-fidelity stage.

Duration

Three weeks

November - December 2022

Problem

Travelers need a way to easily identify and plan their itineraries and find hotels within walkable distance to those identifies activities and attractions.

Solution

Flew the Coop Adventures creates customized get-away itineraries for empty nest travelers based on their “wish list” and budget to take the pains out of researching and planning so they can spend more time doing.

Identifying Needs of the Users

To clearly identify the problem, want, or need users have related to travel, I developed a research plan before beginning any design work. My research included both primary and secondary research methods.

Research Findings

Industry Research

The travel industry is slowly returning to pre-COVID numbers and expected to return to its prepandemic peak of $313.1 billion by 2027. Additionally, travelers like technology that helps them with planning trips.

User Research

Users seem most concerned about overall cost as the driving factor for travel. The overall budget for the trip drove the decisions regarding travel method, type of lodging, and length of stay.

User Insight

Users need help learning about new cities and assistance finding accommodations within walkable distances to activities and attractions they enjoy because they want to spend less time searching and planning and more time doing.

This user insight helped for the user persona for Stephie Penshell, and empty nester who enjoys long weekends away with her husband exploring new places.

Competitor Analysis

Through analysis of the apps available to users, I discovered each competitor offered something, but none fulfilled what users were looking for when planning their travel.

Threats

A lot of apps in this space

Opportunities

Build itineraries based on robust profile

Verified reviews by users

Easy map function to help find walkable hotel

Ad based funding model vs. Fee for service

Prototyping Flew the Coop

I took what I learned from my research to begin the app design.

Sketches

The sketches below focused on entering the app and establishing a robust profile.

Wireframing

After completing the sketches I used Figma to build wireframes to conduct user testing.

User Testing

User tests were performed to see if users could create an account using email without encountering any issues.

Users found the process needed explaining to complete. They noted that it felt like a step or more was missing. Users also noted that the process did not seem intuitive, and they were not sure what to do next.

Final Low Fidelity Prototype

Additional user testing identified users want the ability to use filters built into a profile that allow for selecting destinations, activities, and creating itineraries. These results lead to the development of the final prototype.

Final Thoughts and Next Steps

For future iterations and other projects, I plan to look into the industry more at the start of the project to help gain insights from trends and how they relate to consumer problem/wants/needs.

I would also remember that sometimes beginning again is better than forcing a solution or change into an unworkable draft. As long as you are learning and growing, it is not a failure but an opportunity for improvement.
